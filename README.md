# Thungela Resources: Financial & Capital Allocation Analysis (2021-2025)

**Analyst:** Harry 
**Project Type:** Corporate Finance / Financial Modeling

## 📌 Objective
This project analyzes the 5-year post-demerger financial performance of Thungela Resources Ltd. Following its successful spin-off from Anglo American in June 2021 to become a pure-play thermal coal producer, this model tracks the company's trajectory from its foundational base year, through the 2022 energy market peak, down to the margin compression of 2025[cite: 1]. 

## 📊 Data Source
All raw data was extracted, normalized, and modeled manually from Thungela Resources Ltd. Audited Annual Financial Statements (2021-2025). 
* **Source:** [Thungela Investor Relations: Annual Reports](https://www.thungela.com/investors/annual-reports)

## 💡 Executive Summary & Strategic Insights
By bridging the income statement to free cash flow generation, several key operational and macroeconomic narratives emerged[cite: 1]:

* **The Commodity Volatility Cycle:** Mapped the revenue impact of benchmark thermal coal peaking at USD 270.87/t in 2022 during the global energy crisis, followed by a sharp correction and compression by 2025[cite: 1].
* **The Margin Squeeze:** Modeled the deterioration of Adjusted EBITDA margins from a high of 58% in 2022 down to 4% in 2025 as operational costs remained sticky against falling realized export prices[cite: 1].
* **Logistical Bottlenecks:** Identified the impact of Transnet Freight Rail (TFR) underperformance, which trapped cash in working capital and increased stockpile inventory from ZAR 3.1B in 2022 to ZAR 4.0B in 2023[cite: 1].
* **Shareholder Return Commitment:** Visualized management's aggressive dividend policy, notably the strategic decision to execute a 422% dividend payout ratio in 2025 by utilizing the massive liquidity buffer built during the 2022 boom[cite: 1].
